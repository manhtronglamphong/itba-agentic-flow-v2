# KA4 — Strategy Analysis

## Purpose

Strategy Analysis describes the BA work that must occur to **understand the
business need, organize and define the change required to meet that need, and
align the resulting strategy with higher-level and lower-level strategies**. It
focuses on the **strategic / tactical** rather than the operational level.

## Description

Strategy is **the approach an enterprise takes to position itself**. Strategy
Analysis defines the **future state** the enterprise wants to reach, examines
the **current state** it is starting from, and the **transition states** in
between. The principal artifact is the **change strategy** — the path from
current to future state — supported by a **business case** and an explicit
**risk posture**.

Strategy Analysis is performed at any time the BA needs to investigate a new
business need; it is **not** confined to project initiation.

## Tasks

This KA defines four tasks. Each task lists its **explicit BABOK v3 Inputs,
Elements, Guidelines/Tools, Techniques, Stakeholders, and Outputs**. Do not
add or remove items: this is the canonical taxonomy.

### Task 4.1 — Analyze Current State

**Purpose.** Understand the reasons why an enterprise needs to change, the
elements of the enterprise likely to be impacted, and the relationships
between those elements.

**Description.** No change is undertaken in a vacuum. The current state is
established to provide the **baseline** against which the future state is
defined and the change strategy is assessed. The current state is documented
in just enough detail to justify the change.

**Inputs.**
- **Elicitation Results (any state)** — useful information about the business need or current state.
- **Needs** *(optional; may come from prior work and be refined here)*.

**Elements.**

2. Organizational Structure and Culture
3. Capabilities and Processes

8. External Influencers

### Task 4.2 — Define Future State

**Purpose.** Determine the set of necessary conditions to meet the business need.

**Description.** The future state defines a **set of changes to capabilities,
processes, technologies, etc.** that will achieve the desired outcome. The
future state must be feasible, valuable, and bounded.

**Inputs.**

### Task 4.3 — Assess Risks

**Purpose.** Understand the undesirable consequences of internal and external
forces on the enterprise during the change and after it is implemented; then
recommend a course of action.

**Description.** Risks are identified, analyzed for probability and impact,
prioritized, and assigned an explicit response (avoid, transfer, mitigate,
accept, increase for upside).

**Inputs.**
- **Business Objectives** — the targets against which risks are measured.
- **Elicitation Results (confirmed)** — the validated body of facts on which risk identification draws.
- **Influences** *(elicited; internal and external)* — forces acting on the change.
- **Potential Value** — the upside that risks may erode.
- **Requirements (any state)** *(optional)* — risks attached to specific requirements.

**Elements.**
1. Unknowns
2. Constraints, Assumptions, and Dependencies
3. Negative Impact to Value
4. Risk Tolerance
5. Recommendation

**Guidelines and Tools.**

### Task 4.4 — Define Change Strategy

**Purpose.** Develop and assess alternative approaches to the change, then
select the recommended approach.

**Description.** The change strategy describes **how the enterprise will move
from the current state to the future state**. It is informed by the current
state, future state, and risk posture, and is justified by a **business case**.

**Inputs.**

- **Future State Description**
- **Risk Analysis Results**

## Common Failure Modes

- **Skipping 4.1** (jumping to a future state with no baseline) → unmeasurable change.
- **4.2 written as a list of features** → confuses Future State with Solution.
- **4.3 without explicit risk tolerance** → produces a list, not a posture.
- **4.4 with one option** → no real decision was made; the business case is theatre.

## Dev-Ready Handoffs

- **Change Strategy** + **Solution Scope** → consumed by **KA5** to produce
  Solution-level requirements and designs.
- **Business Case** (instrumented via Task 4.4) → ongoing input to **KA6**
  Solution Evaluation for value realization tracking.
