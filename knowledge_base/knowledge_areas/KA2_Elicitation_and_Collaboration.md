# KA2 — Elicitation and Collaboration

## Purpose
Describes how BAs work with stakeholders to **identify, understand, and confirm** their information needs, and how those stakeholders are kept engaged.

## Description
Elicitation is the *act* of drawing information out; collaboration is the *ongoing relationship* that makes elicitation possible and sustainable.

## Tasks

### Task 2.1 — Prepare for Elicitation
**Purpose.** Understand the scope of the elicitation activity, select techniques, and plan logistics.
**Inputs.** Needs; Stakeholder Engagement Approach.
**Elements.** Understand the Scope of Elicitation; Select Elicitation Techniques; Set Up Logistics; Secure Supporting Materials; Prepare Stakeholders.
**Outputs.** **Elicitation Activity Plan**.

### Task 2.2 — Conduct Elicitation
**Purpose.** Draw out information from stakeholders.
**Inputs.** Elicitation Activity Plan.
**Elements.** Guide Elicitation Activity; Capture Elicitation Outcomes (three types: Collaborative, Research, Experiment).
**Outputs.** **Elicitation Results (unconfirmed)**.

### Task 2.3 — Confirm Elicitation Results
**Purpose.** Check the information gathered for accuracy and consistency.
**Inputs.** Elicitation Results (unconfirmed).
**Elements.** Compare Elicitation Results Against Source Information; Compare Elicitation Results Against Other Elicitation Results.
**Outputs.** **Elicitation Results (confirmed)**.

### Task 2.4 — Communicate Business Analysis Information
**Purpose.** Ensure stakeholders share a common understanding of BA information.
**Inputs.** BA Information; Stakeholder Engagement Approach.
**Elements.** Determine Objectives and Format of Communication; Communicate BA Package.
**Outputs.** **Business Analysis Information (communicated)**.

### Task 2.5 — Manage Stakeholder Collaboration
**Purpose.** Encourage stakeholders to work toward a common goal.
**Inputs.** Stakeholder Engagement Approach; BA Performance Assessment.
**Elements.** Gain Agreement on Commitments; Monitor Stakeholder Engagement; Collaboration.
**Outputs.** **Stakeholder Engagement** (ongoing collaboration outcomes).

## Representative Techniques
Benchmarking and Market Analysis; Brainstorming; Business Rules Analysis; Collaborative Games; Concept Modelling; Data Mining; Data Modelling; Document Analysis; Focus Groups; Interface Analysis; Interviews; Mind Mapping; Observation; Process Analysis; Process Modelling; Prototyping; Survey or Questionnaire; Workshops.

## Typical Stakeholders
Any group with information relevant to the change — particularly Customer, Domain SME, End User, Implementation SME, Regulator, Sponsor.

## BACCM Linkage
- **Stakeholder** is the central concept.
- **Need** is the artifact being elicited.
- **Context** shapes which elicitation techniques are viable.
